02

Before You Enter

Professionalism starts before the patient ever sees you.

Preparation checklist

  • Professionalism. Dress the part; have your exam tools ready.
  • Identification. Wear your ID; carry note-taking materials.
  • Review the chart. Read the patient's record thoroughly in advance.
  • Interpreter? Check whether a language interpreter is needed.
  • Self-check. Make sure you're mentally present and focused.

The first moments in the room

  1. Sanitize. Hand hygiene the moment you walk in.
  2. Introduce. State your name and clinical role clearly.
  3. Give the roadmap. Outline how the visit will flow:
    Interview about you and your visitPhysical examinationTeam collaboration to finalize a plan
  4. Engage. Hold eye contact and stay present.

04

Complaint vs. Concern

The medical reason for the visit isn't always what the patient is actually afraid of.

Chief Complaint

The medical reason

The clinical problem that brought them in — stated in symptom terms.

"Shortness of breath."
Chief Concern / PPI

The personal fear

The patient's own theory or worry about what's happening — their perspective on the illness.

"Do I have lung cancer?"

Catching the concern beneath the complaint is what turns a transaction into patient-centered care. Ask for it directly.

06

The Histories

The layered background that reveals risk and context.

Medical & Surgical

Chronic conditions (diabetes, hypertension), past procedures with dates, and immunization status (flu, COVID-19, age-appropriate vaccines).

Medications

For every drug, capture four things. Include prescriptions, OTC, supplements, and vitamins — and ask directed questions, since patients forget.

NAMEDOSEROUTEFREQUENCY

Allergies

Record the specific substance and the reaction it causes — the reaction is what matters clinically.

Penicillin → anaphylaxis
Strawberries → hives

Family & Social

Family: close relatives and grandparents for hereditary risk (heart disease, stroke, cancer). Social: smoking (pack-years), alcohol, drugs, living situation. Handle sensitive topics with reassurance of privacy.
